01

The investment thesis

Context

From pandemic peak to structural reset

Nike's post-COVID collapse is not a single story but four compounding failures occurring simultaneously: a misguided DTC acceleration that alienated wholesale partners; an innovation drought that ceded ground to On, Hoka, and New Balance; a structural deterioration in Greater China; and a CEO — John Donahoe — who came from eBay and never fully understood that Nike is, at its core, a sport and culture brand. The market has priced in permanent impairment. The pitch is that it's pricing in too much permanence.

The fall
–65%
Stock decline since start of 2022

Nike fell 29.8% in 2022, 7.2% in 2023, and 30.3% in 2024 — the second-worst Dow component in 2024. June 28, 2024 was the worst single trading day in company history: –21% in one session, wiping $28B in market cap.

The opportunity
+35%
Consensus upside to analyst PT of $59–$62

36 analysts cover NKE. The majority sit at Hold, not Sell — meaning the debate is "when does this recover?" not "does it recover?" Any EPS stabilization plus multiple re-rating creates a double-whammy upside that isn't priced in.

DTC blunder — the core mistake

They focused on WHERE they sold, not WHAT they sold

Wedbush analysts nailed it: "The company's original pre-COVID 'Consumer Direct' plan was doing just fine, but by accelerating it in 2020, they focused too much on WHERE they were selling and lost focus of WHAT they were selling." Nike cut hundreds of wholesale partners — Big 5, Dunham's, Zappos, Urban Outfitters, Dillard's — and their shelf space was immediately occupied by On, Hoka, and New Balance.

Competitive disruption

On + Hoka filled the void Nike left

When Nike pulled back from wholesale, On and Hoka didn't just get shelf space — they got credibility. On grew from $330M to $1.8B revenue between 2020–2025. Hoka grew from $352M to $1.4B. Combined they went from $682M to $3.2B, a near 5x increase — directly exploiting the gap Nike created.

China — structural vs. cyclical?

The single biggest unresolved question

Greater China fell from 18.6% of revenue in 2021 to 14.2% in 2025, and is now declining 17% per quarter. Anta's market share rose from 14% to 19% since 2019, Li-Ning from 6% to 9%. The "Guochao" (national pride) movement is real. The question is whether Nike can re-localize fast enough — their new Shanghai R&D lab and "for China, in China" strategy is a direct response.

03

Elliott Hill's "Win Now" turnaround strategy

Hill (Nike lifer, returned Oct 2024 from retirement) stated bluntly: "We lost our obsession with sport." His strategy has seven distinct pillars, each with measurable early indicators.

🏪
Wholesale rebuild
Reversed the DTC-only strategy. Returned to Amazon (6-yr hiatus), re-engaged DSW, Macy's, Urban Outfitters, Zappos. Wholesale grew +8% in Q2 FY2026. Hill personally met with retail partners to rebuild trust. Order book improving sequentially — holiday orders up.
Early progress
⚡
Sport Offense innovation engine
Unified Nike, Jordan, and Converse R&D (~1,000 designers) into a single unit. Launched four new platforms: Aero-FIT (2x airflow apparel), Nike Mind (neuroscience-backed footwear), Air Milano, and Project Amplify (world's first motor-assisted running shoe). Phil McCartney appointed Chief Innovation Officer.
Pipeline, not revenue yet
👟
NikeSKIMS — women's market
Joint venture with Kim Kardashian's SKIMS launched Spring 2025. Targets the women's activewear segment where Lululemon has dominated. Collection sold out in North America; global rollout in 2026. Addresses a historically underpenetrated segment for Nike.
Strong early demand
💰
Pricing power recovery
Stopped the discounting that eroded brand premium and gross margins. Nike footwear prices rose 17%, apparel 14%, equipment 18% from Sept 2024–2025. Surgical price hikes of $2–$10 on adult lines citing tariffs, while exempting Air Force 1 and kids' gear to protect brand loyalty.
Margin signal positive
⚽
2026 World Cup catalyst
Tournament hosted in USA/Canada/Mexico — Nike's home soil. Sponsors 12 national teams including Brazil ($100M/yr through 2038), France, England, USA. Analysts project $1.0–1.3B revenue boost. Marketing spend hitting $5B+. "Rip the Script" campaign and 12-week rolling content strategy.
Adidas outperforming on football
🇨🇳
China localization ("For China, in China")
Opened Shanghai R&D lab. Launched Douyin flagship (years behind competitors). Locally tailored product lines to combat Anta and Li-Ning. But revenue declined 17% in Q2 FY2026 — the 6th consecutive quarterly decline. CEO Hill admitted turnaround "taking longer than expected."
Highest risk pillar
🏗️
Org restructuring for speed
Eliminated CCO and CTO roles. Four geographic presidents now report directly to Hill. New COO (Venkatesh Alagirisamy) oversees tech + supply chain. Removed management layers to accelerate decision-making. ~77,800 employees globally; headcount reduced via layoffs in early 2024.
Speed vs. knowledge risk

The Nike valuation paradox. Nike trades at ~28x trailing P/E on depressed EPS of $1.52. Competitive moat — Porter's Five Forces

Nike has a weakened but real moat. The DTC blunder exposed that the moat is narrower than the market assumed at $181 — but brand equity and athlete network remain structurally intact. The investment thesis hinges on whether the operational moat (execution, distribution, innovation pipeline) can be rebuilt before insurgents permanently compound their gains.

Threat of new entrants
Low–Med
  • $1B+ athlete roster (Brazil $100M/yr through 2038, LeBron, Serena) is multi-decade capital that cannot be replicated quickly — Nike 10-K FY2025, contractual commitments footnote
  • Manufacturing scale across Vietnam/Indonesia locks in cost advantages vs. new entrants who must pay spot pricing
  • Air cushioning IP portfolio spans 300+ patents — a genuine technical barrier in performance footwear
  • On Holding scaled from $330M → $1.8B in 5 years by attacking a single category (performance running), proving DTC + specialty retail can sidestep Nike's distribution moat — On FY2025 Annual Report
  • Low capital barrier for fashion/lifestyle insurgents via dropship + Shopify + Instagram — the "brand moat" matters less in trend cycles
Sources: Nike 10-K FY2025; On Holding FY2025 Annual Report
Threat of substitutes
Medium ↑
  • Performance running substitution is credible and growing: On, Hoka, Brooks, Asics all gained measurable unit share in the running specialty channel Nike abandoned 2020–2022 — Running USA 2024 Industry Survey
  • In women's apparel, Lululemon holds structural substitution advantage — Nike's own NikeSKIMS JV is an admission that organic substitution risk is real — PRNewswire, Feb 18 2025
  • In China, Anta and Li-Ning offer culturally aligned substitutes with comparable or superior technology for local consumers; Anta's market share rose from 14% → 19% since 2019 — Front Office Sports, Nov 2024
  • In lifestyle/Jordan/Dunk segment, cultural cachet remains near-irreplaceable — resale premiums on Nike retros still multiples of retail price
  • Switching costs are low (shoes are shoes) — brand loyalty is the only friction, and it has weakened measurably in the 18–34 male demographic
Sources: Running USA 2024; Front Office Sports Nov 2024; NikeSKIMS PRNewswire
Bargaining power of suppliers
Low
  • Nike is the single largest customer for most of its Tier-1 factories in Vietnam and Indonesia — suppliers don't set terms — Nike 10-K FY2025, Sourcing section
  • Top 5 contract manufacturers represent ~50% of Nike's production; none are likely to walk given the volume dependency
  • Nike's "Considered Design" and lean manufacturing programs embed Nike engineers into factory processes, creating mutual lock-in
  • 95% offshore concentration creates tariff and geopolitical exposure that suppliers cannot solve — this is a policy risk, not a supplier power risk, but it manifests through the supply chain — Nike 10-K FY2025, Risk Factors
  • Supply chain diversification (shifting some production to India/Morocco) is underway but multi-year; near-term, Nike is exposed to Vietnam tariff rates of 46% before any negotiated exclusions
Source: Nike 10-K FY2025 (Sourcing, Risk Factors sections)
Bargaining power of buyers (wholesale)
Medium ↑
  • Nike's 2020–2022 wholesale pullback cost it retailer goodwill and shelf space — when it returned to Amazon, DSW, and Macy's in 2025, those retailers had proven On/Hoka alternatives and negotiated from a stronger position — Retail Dive, Mar 2024
  • Foot Locker publicly flagged Nike dependency risk in 2023 and diversified its buy toward On and New Balance — Nike's ~65% share of Foot Locker's floor dropped — Foot Locker FY2023 Annual Report
  • Nike's brand still drives traffic to retail partners — Foot Locker needs Nike more than the inverse, at the aggregate level
  • Wholesale grew +8% in Q2 FY2026 — early evidence that the relationship rebuild is working — Nike 8-K Q2 FY2026
  • Holiday 2025 order book is reportedly improving sequentially — positive leading indicator but not yet confirmed in reported revenue
Sources: Retail Dive Mar 2024; Foot Locker FY2023 Annual Report; Nike 8-K Q2 FY2026
Competitive rivalry
High ↑
  • Adidas under Bjørn Gulden is fully recovered: +10% revenue growth in FY2025, Sambas/Gazelles dominating lifestyle, and Adidas is the official FIFA World Cup sponsor — outmaneuvered Nike on the biggest near-term marketing event — Adidas FY2025 Annual Report
  • On Holding +30% revenue growth FY2025; added Roger Federer, Iga Swiatek, Zendaya partnerships targeting the exact prestige consumer Nike has historically owned — On FY2025 Annual Report
  • New Balance lifestyle revival (990 series, Teddy Santis creative direction) has taken meaningful street-wear share from Jordan and Dunk franchises
  • In China: Anta Sports +9% revenue, Li-Ning gaining ground with Guochao (national pride) positioning; combined they now hold ~28% of China sportswear vs. Nike's declining share — Front Office Sports Nov 2024
  • No single competitor has Nike's global scale, multi-sport presence, and brand recognition simultaneously — rivalry is intense in each category but still fragmented against Nike's full portfolio
Sources: Adidas FY2025; On FY2025; Front Office Sports Nov 2024
Overall moat verdict
Narrow
  • Still intact: Brand storytelling + cultural moment ownership (no competitor can do a Super Bowl halftime globally)
  • Still intact: Athlete network depth — Brazil $100M/yr through 2038, France, England, USA national teams, LeBron, Serena, Kylian Mbappé
  • Still intact: Air technology IP and manufacturing scale produce genuine cost/performance advantages in premium footwear
  • Eroded: Distribution moat — DTC-only bet destroyed wholesale relationships; rebuilding takes years not quarters
  • Eroded: Innovation moat — On and Hoka now set the pace in performance running; Nike is catching up, not leading
  • Eroded: China moat — Guochao movement is structural; Nike's "for China, in China" strategy is 3–5 years behind Anta's localization
Assessment based on Nike 10-K FY2025, Q3 FY2026 10-Q, and competitor filings cited above
Moat summary — what the pitch assumes
Moat elements still intact (support the bull case)
  • Brand equity: Nike's Interbrand value ~$30B — #1 apparel brand globally for 28 consecutive years. No competitor has dislodged this at the aggregate level.
  • Jordan Brand: $7B+ sub-brand growing independently; cultural cachet in streetwear/basketball unchallenged by any insurgent.
  • Athlete contracts: $100M/yr Brazil deal through 2038; France, England, USA national team sponsorships create multi-decade marketing infrastructure.
  • Financial durability: $8B+ cash, investment-grade balance sheet, 24 consecutive years of dividend growth — moat wide enough to fund a multi-year recovery.
Moat elements that eroded (create the risk case)
  • Distribution: Pulling from 400+ wholesale partners created permanent shelf-space losses that On and Hoka converted into credibility — not just revenue.
  • Performance running: Hoka owns the specialty running channel; Brooks and On own premium performance positioning. Nike has not shipped a category-defining running shoe since Vaporfly.
  • China execution: Opening a Douyin flagship in 2024 — years after Anta and Li-Ning built native digital ecosystems — is too slow. Revenue has declined six consecutive quarters.
  • Women's: Lululemon built a $11B women's activewear empire in the space Nike under-served for a decade. NikeSKIMS is an admission of failure, not a solution.

Key risks to the thesis

High severity
China is structurally impaired, not cyclical
If the "Guochao" movement and Anta/Li-Ning's technology gains are permanent, Greater China (~14% of revenue) never recovers to prior levels. Six consecutive quarterly declines with no floor in sight. Nike's "for China, in China" strategy is nascent — they only opened a Douyin store in 2024, years behind competitors. BNP Paribas: "We don't see growth in Nike China for the next few years."
High severity
Tariff exposure — 95% manufactured offshore
Vietnam (51% of footwear), Indonesia (28%), China (17%) all face elevated US tariffs. Nike flagged ~$1B in annual tariff costs. Without full price pass-through or supply chain diversification, this directly compresses margins that are already 190bps below pre-crisis levels. Diversification takes years — not quarters.
Medium severity
Innovation pipeline arrives too late
Project Amplify and Nike Mind are "years away" from commercial launch. On and Hoka continue shipping innovative products every season. The window for Nike to reclaim the performance running category may narrow faster than the innovation pipeline can deliver. Aero-FIT and Air Milano are apparel — not the core running footwear battlefield.
Medium severity
World Cup disappoints vs. lofty expectations
Adidas is the official World Cup sponsor, supplies the match ball, and sponsors 14 teams vs. Nike's 12. JD Sports reports Adidas jerseys (Argentina, Mexico) are outselling Nike's. RBC downgraded Nike one day before the opening match, citing "recovery progressing slower than market expects." $5B marketing spend is a bet, not a guarantee.
Lower severity
Wholesale rebuild cannibalizes DTC margins
Wholesale margins (30–35%) are structurally lower than DTC (50%+). As Nike re-engages Amazon, DSW, Macy's — revenue recovers but margin mix deteriorates. The model needs both volume AND pricing discipline to work simultaneously.
Lower severity
Securities fraud litigation overhang
Deka Investment GmbH and Caisse de dépôt (CDPQ) filed a nearly 300-page amended complaint alleging Nike executives falsely assured investors the DTC strategy was working while internal red flags showed persistent failure. Settlement risk and management distraction.
